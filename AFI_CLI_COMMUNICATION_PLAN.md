# AFI CLI Communication Plan

> **Status: RETIRED (2026-06-19)** — Aspirational only; not an active mandate.

## Overview

This communication plan ensures transparent, timely, and effective information sharing among CLI stakeholders, developers, and the broader AFI community. It supports the governance framework and facilitates successful adoption of CLI standards.

## Status Reports

### Weekly Status Reports
- **Frequency:** Every Friday
- **Audience:** CLI maintainers, governance committee
- **Content:**
  - Progress on active CLI projects
  - Blockers and resolutions
  - Upcoming reviews and approvals
  - Compliance status updates
- **Format:** Email summary with dashboard links
- **Distribution:** Internal mailing list

### Monthly Executive Reports
- **Frequency:** First business day of month
- **Audience:** AFI leadership, product owners
- **Content:**
  - Key achievements and milestones
  - Risk assessment and mitigation plans
  - Resource utilization and budget status
  - Roadmap progress and adjustments
- **Format:** 2-page executive summary with metrics
- **Distribution:** Leadership mailing list

### Quarterly Comprehensive Reports
- **Frequency:** End of quarter
- **Audience:** All stakeholders
- **Content:**
  - Full compliance audit results
  - Performance metrics and KPIs
  - Stakeholder feedback analysis
  - Strategic recommendations
  - Annual roadmap updates
- **Format:** Detailed report with charts and appendices
- **Distribution:** Published to internal wiki and shared drives

### Ad-hoc Reports
- **Triggers:** Major incidents, significant changes, stakeholder requests
- **Timeline:** Within 24 hours of trigger
- **Format:** Situation-specific, focused on impact and resolution
- **Distribution:** As appropriate for the situation

## Stakeholder Engagement

- **Monthly Stakeholder Meetings:** Open forum for questions and feedback
- **Quarterly Town Halls:** Major updates and roadmap discussions

### Feedback Mechanisms
- **Suggestion Box:** Anonymous feedback form
- **Retrospectives:** After major initiatives

## Training Program

### Developer Training Schedule
- **New Hire Orientation:** Mandatory 2-hour session on CLI standards
- **Monthly Workshops:** Deep dives on specific topics (frameworks, security, testing)
- **Quarterly Advanced Sessions:** Best practices and advanced techniques
- **Annual Conference:** Full-day training event with external speakers

### Certification Requirements
- **CLI Developer Certification:**
  - Prerequisites: 3 months experience or completion of training program
  - Components: Written exam, practical coding assessment, peer review
  - Validity: 2 years, recertification required
  - Benefits: Authority to approve minor changes, committee voting rights

- **CLI Maintainer Certification:**
  - Prerequisites: CLI Developer certification + 6 months maintenance experience
  - Components: Advanced exam, security assessment, leadership evaluation
  - Validity: 3 years
  - Benefits: Authority for major approvals, committee membership eligibility

### Training Resources
- **Online Learning Platform:** Self-paced courses on CLI standards
- **Video Library:** Recorded sessions and tutorials
- **Mentorship Program:** Experienced developers guide newcomers
- **Documentation:** Comprehensive guides and cheat sheets

### Training Metrics
- **Completion Rates:** > 90% for mandatory training
- **Certification Rates:** > 80% of eligible developers certified
- **Satisfaction Scores:** > 4.0/5.0 in post-training surveys
- **Knowledge Retention:** > 85% in follow-up assessments

## Communication Channels and Tools

### Primary Channels
- **Email:** Formal announcements and reports
- **Slack:** Daily communication and quick questions
- **GitHub:** Technical discussions and code reviews
- **Wiki:** Documentation and knowledge base

### Tools and Platforms
- **Video Conferencing:** Zoom for meetings and training
- **Project Management:** JIRA for tracking and reporting
- **Documentation:** Confluence for collaborative writing
- **Survey Tools:** Google Forms for feedback collection

### Communication Guidelines
- **Tone:** Professional, collaborative, solution-oriented
- **Timeliness:** Respond within 24 hours for routine, 4 hours for urgent
- **Transparency:** Share both successes and challenges
- **Inclusivity:** Use accessible language and formats

## Crisis Communication

### Incident Response
- **Trigger:** Security incidents, major outages, compliance violations
- **Immediate Actions:**
  - Notify security lead and committee chair
  - Assess impact and containment
  - Communicate to affected stakeholders
- **Communication Plan:**
  - Initial notification within 1 hour
  - Hourly updates during active incident
  - Post-incident report within 24 hours
  - Lessons learned shared within 1 week

### Escalation Protocol
- **Level 1:** Team lead handles, inform committee
- **Level 2:** Committee chair involved, leadership notified
- **Level 3:** Full leadership team, external communication if needed

### Media and External Communication
- **Spokesperson:** Designated committee member
- **Key Messages:** Focus on impact, resolution timeline, prevention measures
- **Channels:** Internal first, external only if required
