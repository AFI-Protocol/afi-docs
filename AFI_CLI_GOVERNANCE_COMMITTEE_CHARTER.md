# AFI CLI Governance Committee Charter

> **Status: RETIRED (2026-06-19)** — Aspirational only; not an active mandate. See [`specs/audit/AFI_CLI_FRAMEWORKS_DECISIONS.md`](./specs/audit/AFI_CLI_FRAMEWORKS_DECISIONS.md).

## Purpose

The AFI CLI Governance Committee is responsible for overseeing the standardization, maintenance, and evolution of Command Line Interface (CLI) tools across all AFI project repositories. The committee ensures consistency, quality, and alignment with AFI's technical and security standards while facilitating collaboration among CLI developers and stakeholders.

## Scope

This charter covers:
- CLI framework standards and shared components
- Review and approval of new CLI proposals
- Maintenance and deprecation policies
- Migration strategies for existing CLIs
- Quality assurance and compliance monitoring
- Training and documentation standards

## Committee Composition

### Membership
The committee consists of 5-7 members with diverse expertise:
- **Chair**: Oversees committee operations and represents CLI governance to AFI leadership
- **Technical Lead**: Provides technical expertise on CLI frameworks and standards
- **Security Representative**: Ensures security compliance in CLI development
- **Developer Representatives** (2-3): Active CLI developers from different repositories
- **Product Owner Representative**: Represents stakeholder needs and priorities

- Chair: [To be appointed by AFI Tech Leadership]

- Terms: 1 year, renewable
- Rotation: Staggered to maintain continuity
- Appointment: By AFI Tech Leadership with committee input

## Responsibilities

### Strategic Oversight
- Define and maintain CLI standards and best practices
- Monitor industry trends and incorporate relevant advancements
- Establish policies for framework updates and deprecations

### Review and Approval
- Review CLI proposals for compliance with standards
- Approve major changes to shared frameworks
- Conduct security and quality reviews

### Maintenance and Support
- Oversee maintenance schedules and procedures
- Coordinate training programs for CLI developers
- Monitor compliance and address violations

### Communication and Reporting
- Publish quarterly status reports
- Maintain communication plan with stakeholders
- Facilitate knowledge sharing among CLI teams

## Authority

### Decision-Making
- **Consensus**: Preferred for standard decisions
- **Majority Vote**: For contentious issues
- **Escalation**: To AFI Tech Leadership for blocking decisions

### Enforcement
- **Recommendations**: For minor compliance issues
- **Mandates**: For critical security or standards violations
- **Appeals**: Process available through AFI governance system

### Resource Allocation
- Approve budget for CLI infrastructure improvements
- Prioritize maintenance tasks and feature requests
- Allocate committee time and resources

## Meetings and Operations

### Regular Meetings
- **Weekly Status**: Wednesdays, 30 minutes, focus on blockers and updates
- **Monthly Strategy**: First Wednesday, 1 hour, strategic planning
- **Quarterly Review**: End of quarter, 2 hours, comprehensive assessment

### Decision Timeline
- **Routine Reviews**: 1-2 weeks
- **Major Decisions**: 2-4 weeks with stakeholder input
- **Emergency Issues**: 24-48 hours

### Documentation
- All decisions documented in meeting minutes
- Public charter and procedures maintained in repository
- Annual charter review and updates

## Success Metrics

- 100% compliance with CLI standards within 3 months
- < 24 hour response time to critical issues
- > 90% test coverage maintained across all CLIs
- Positive stakeholder feedback on governance processes

## Amendments

This charter may be amended by:
- Committee consensus with 75% approval
- AFI Tech Leadership approval
- Annual review required
